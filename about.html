About Us

Welcome to SAA

Association of Success in Action SAA is a non -profit organization which was registered as local NGO in 20 Dec 2023. It has General Assembly (GA) at the apex. Under its GA, there is board of director then office secretariat that leads the overall activities of the organization. SAA has been established by five founding members’ initiative with taking the leading part by Mr. Mohammed Ahmed to implement sustainable community development programs. Hence, SAA is emerged towards making interventions on issues pertinent to climate change, livelihood, Water Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH), Health, DRR , timely response to emergency and to ensure sustainable resilience to any of unexpected hazards...

History of SAA

SAA was established by 7 Founding members initiative; Mr. Bekeru Furi (MA) the present Executive Director, taking the lead, to implement sustainable community development programs to relieve poverty, and to improve the food security status of the community through supporting the income-generating activity l protection and rehabilitation and pro-poor program objectives. Thus, SAA emerged to make the humanitarian.
While continuing to expand these interventions; the organization currently, has shown a greater leap in multiple engagement including Emergency Response, disaster risk preparedness, life-saving, and resilience-building programs, humanitarian programs, community empowerment, governance/social accountability, gender, Water Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH), Energy and health services, and education, etc.

Organizational Overview

Executive Summary

Vision

See Ethiopian youth turned down from poverty, backwardness, and all other social injustices and miseries and remain it with pages of history.

Mission

Create conducive working opportunities by raising awareness, and improving understanding, To raise awareness improve knowledge and understanding of poor youth of Ethiopia about harmful practices & small-scale businesses in order to tackle poverty.

Purpose

The requested fund is intended to render marketable vocational skill training in Ethiopia To equip poor women to become economically productive & self-Supportive in a sustainable manner in the mentioned country.

Target Population

Arranged for humanitarian, rehabilitation youth who recently organized in micro-business, but were not able to start production (work) due to lack of financial support.

Organizational Structure and Leadership

SAA's organizational structure includes a General Assembly, a board of directors, and a secretariat. The secretariat comprises 83 skilled professionals with Master's degrees and 20 technical workers, overseeing the organization's activities.

Objectives SAA

  • To expand the environmental protection, development land-rehabilitation programs to enhance employment and better future of the host, refugees and IDP communiqués
  • To introduce an inclusive livelihood development schemes to specifically benefit the of vulnerable groups of the community .
  • To create safe access to alternative source of energy and energy for cooking, lighting and productive use
  • To engage in energy and energy efficiency and reforestation programs with the objective to carbon emission reduction and climate change
  • To enhance an integrated life-saving and resilience building, Emergency Response, disaster risk preparedness, community empowerment, governance/social accountability, gender and gender in equality plans/programs for the host, refugees IDPs and the vulnerable groups
  • To provide access to clean and potable water, Water Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH), Energy, Shelter and NFI, health and education services, etc.
  • To increase household income and economic activities through on-farm as well off-farm economic/income sources programs.
  • To create knowledge transfer and skill development platforms for the community to assist it exercise its decision-making rights in development as well in asking/keeping its rights and responsibilities
  • To enhance capacities, skills and preparedness of the community to withstand and manage drought & disaster risks
