Organizational Overview

Executive Summary

Vision

See Ethiopian youth rise above poverty, backwardness, and social injustices, leaving a lasting mark in history.

Mission

To create opportunities by raising awareness and improving the knowledge and understanding of poor Ethiopian youth, tackling poverty and harmful practices.

Purpose

To provide marketable vocational skill training in Ethiopia, equipping poor women to become economically productive and self-supportive sustainably.

Target Population

For humanitarian rehabilitation of youth who recently organized micro-businesses but lack financial support to start production.

Organizational Structure and Leadership

SAA's organizational structure includes a General Assembly, a board of directors, and a secretariat. The secretariat comprises 83 skilled professionals with Master's degrees and 20 technical workers, overseeing the organization's activities.

Objectives SAA

  • Expand Environmental Protection

    To expand environmental protection and development land-rehabilitation programs to enhance employment and create a better future for host communities, refugees, and IDPs.

  • Introduce Inclusive Livelihood Development Schemes

    To introduce inclusive livelihood development schemes specifically benefiting vulnerable groups in the community.

  • Provide Access to Alternative Energy Sources

    To create safe access to alternative sources of energy for cooking, lighting, and productive use.

  • Engage in Energy Efficiency and Reforestation

    To engage in energy efficiency and reforestation programs with the objective of carbon emission reduction and addressing climate change.

  • Enhance Resilience Building and Community Empowerment

    To enhance integrated life-saving and resilience building, emergency response, disaster risk preparedness, community empowerment, governance, social accountability, and gender equality plans/programs for host communities, refugees, IDPs, and vulnerable groups.

  • Provide Access to Essential Services

    To provide access to clean and potable water, water sanitation and hygiene (WASH), energy, shelter, health, and education services.

  • Increase Household Income and Economic Activities

    To increase household income and economic activities through on-farm and off-farm economic/income sources programs.

  • Create Knowledge Transfer and Skill Development Platforms

    To create knowledge transfer and skill development platforms for the community to exercise its decision-making rights in development and uphold its rights and responsibilities.

  • Enhance Community Capacities and Preparedness

    To enhance capacities, skills, and preparedness of the community to withstand and manage drought and disaster risks.

Core Values SAA

  • Sustainability

    We enable communities to own each project to ensure its sustainability.

  • Trust

    Ensuring transparency at all levels of our work.

  • Proficiency

    Providing creative expertise and strong commitment.

  • Solidarity

    Harmony within our charity organization and with the people we aim to support.

  • Justice

    Fairness within our charity organization as well as in society.

  • Integrity

  • Flexibility

    Showing our willingness to change and learn.
